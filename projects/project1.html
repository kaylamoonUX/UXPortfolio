Project Overview

Problem

Chiropractors often give tasks for their patients in between appointments. These tasks are often forgotten or not prioritized by their patients, thus causing their healing process to take longer.

Lack of Communication

Once a patient leaves the office, there is virtually no communication between the patient and profressional until the patient returns

Not knowing what to do

Many patients forget or are not sure how to follow through with the task they are given. stretches can be intricate and easy to mess up

Apathy

Patients may not realize how important it is to follow through with his or her task until they feel the effects. Without knowing the benefits of their to their health, he or she may not care about the tasks

Goals

To create a smooth process for the chiropractor to assign tasks and provide resources for his or her patients to access easily

Easily Accessible

Patients need to be able to easily find references and instructions for the tasks they are given, and the chiropractor needs to be able to easily upload their information.

Accountability

It is easy to forget or to avoid a task when the only reminder given is from the chiropractor as one is leaving the office. The app needs to notify the users and encourage them to meet their goals.

Establishing Communication

A majority of the healing process occurs away from the office. The app needs a way to establish a way for the chiropractor and patient to communicate throughout the week so the chiropractor can be prepared to help the patient in the most accurate way.

Backbone of Design

Research, Empathize, and define

To understand the target audience's needs, I met with 6 people who have met with a chiropractor regularly and three practicing chiropractors

The Chiropractor

What percentage of your patients are regulars?
Do you think an app that connects to patients would be helpful?
Do you leave patients with tasks in between appointments?
What are some of these tasks? How often do people do them?
Is there a trend in what kind of people normally do them?
Why do you think people don't follow through?

Design Skeleton

Paper Prototypes

Using the task flows, I created a set of paper prototypes. Knowing that chiropractors will be using this program on their computer, I created a desktop prototype out of paper. For the patients, I created an app prototype.

Low-Fi Wireframes

After several rounds of usability tests, I moved onto creating a simple wireframe of the app on Figma. I kept everything in gray scale in order to focus on the value contract and placement of different elements

Hi-Fi Wireframes

Once the tests proved that the users were able to easily accomplish the tasks, I moved on to fully in color designs. I did one more round of user testing to prove to myself that the designs were complete.
